# Legal Contract Review Bot

A case study of how Arturo's legal AI was silently hallucinating across contracts — and how CET's chunk inspection made the failure visible and fixable in minutes instead of weeks.

## The Problem

Arturo's legal AI was hallucinating — not randomly, but **systematically**. Every retrieval was operating in the Marginal zone (0.50–0.65 similarity). The model had no way to know the chunks were unreliable, so it synthesized content from three different contracts — an Employment Contract, an NDA, and a Vendor Agreement — into one confident, wrong answer. The lawyer reviewing the output never knew.

**Smoking gun:** an NDA chunk scored **0.63 (Marginal)** and was retrieved alongside chunks from the Employment Contract and Vendor Agreement. CET's chunk inspection tool made this visible at the message level in 20 minutes. Without CET, this is weeks of debugging.

<Warning title="Run 1 — what cross-document hallucination looks like">
  At a `0.50` threshold, the bot returned a single confident answer that quietly fused clauses from three separate contracts. In a legal context this is especially dangerous: the answer reads as authoritative, cites no source, and the lawyer has no signal that the model stitched together incompatible terms from different agreements.
</Warning>

</Note>

## Key Finding

> No single threshold works for every legal query. The recommended production range is **0.60–0.70** — precise enough to surface real clauses, strict enough to prevent fabrication.
