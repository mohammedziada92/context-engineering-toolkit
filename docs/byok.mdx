# BYOK — Bring Your Own Key

CET does not pay for your LLM usage. This is intentional.

## How It Works

| Step | Who acts |
|---|---|
| You enter your OpenRouter key | You |
| CET validates and encrypts it | CET (Supabase Vault) |
| Pipeline runs using your key | CET backend (server-side only) |
| LLM usage billed | OpenRouter → Your account |
| CET revenue from LLM | $0 |

## Why BYOK?

- **You control costs.** See your usage and credits directly on OpenRouter. No markups.
- **You choose your model.** Three curated models, each optimized for different use cases.
- **CET is free.** The toolkit costs nothing. You only pay OpenRouter for tokens consumed.

## Cost Estimates

| Model | ~Prompt tokens | ~Completion tokens | Estimated cost/run |
|---|---|---|---|
| Claude Sonnet 4.6 | 500 | 200 | ~$0.005 |
| Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview | 500 | 200 | ~$0.002 |
| GLM-5 | 500 | 200 | Included in Z.ai subscription |

## Checking Your Credits

In **Settings → API Keys**, CET shows:
- **Credits remaining** (pulled from OpenRouter in real time)
- **Spent this month** broken down by model
- A **credit bar** that turns amber at 20% remaining and red at 10%

<Warning>
  If your OpenRouter credits hit $0, all pipeline runs and Playground sessions will be disabled until you top up.
</Warning>
